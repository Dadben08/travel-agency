HOW TO RESET YOUR MIND WITH A SELF-CARE RESET TRIP

If you've never actually gone on a self-care reset trip, then you've been missing out! Not only do you get to explore the beauty of this world, but you get the
opportunity to reset your frame of mind if it's grown stale, stuffy, and tired from the burdens of life. Today, GlobalNomad lists a few destinations you just have to visit


NEW MEXICO



New Mexico is a great place to visit if you are looking for a mountainous escape with beautiful lakes. From an affordability point of view, New Mexico is a wonderfully
affordable place to live, with a low tax threshold for family households. What's more, New Mexico was recently ranked as the second kindest state in the country,
which could be just the remedy for you if you're looking for good company and conversation.



ATLANTA



Atlanta is one of those places you'll feel blessed to have seen. In fact, it is remarkable what this larger-than-life city has to offer. Atlanta BeltLine notes that
phenomenal outdoor activities include the development of the BeltLine, which includes 2,000 magnificent public parks to take advantage of. As a vacation rental option,
you're sure to find properties that are within your price range. If not, then there are other avenues to purchasing a holiday home. For example, you
could elect to take out another mortgage on your existing home, or you could leverage a home equity loan to make owning a vacation home possible.



FORT LAUDERDALE



Florida is like a beachside resort that you'll never grow tired of. With a laid-back lifestyle, you can escape the pressures of daily life and really kick back here to behold the natural beauty of sun-kissed
dunes and ocean blues around you. Florida for Boomers points out that Florida also ranks low in the Cost of Living Index, making it an excellent place to settle permanently.



SEATTLE



Seattle is another marvelous destination if you want to explore the unexpected. It has incredible nature and architectural
beauty and has one of the best restaurant scenes in the country. You're also likely to get by comfortably from an affordability point of view.
If you love it so much, you could even buy a vacation rental here to make it official. If you can't afford the usual down payment of around 20% to
secure a property, then consider taking out a second mortgage on your primary home to help finance this. The other option is a home equity
loan that you could use to negotiate finance based on the amount outstanding on your existing mortgage
